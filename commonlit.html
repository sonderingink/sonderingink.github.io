CommonLit Article Recommendation

Early childhood literacy can be difficult because of how crucial student engagement is to retention. Improving early childhood literacy can be especially tough if the content does not allow for many opportunities to reinforce it. However, like some good rest on a sick day, Olivia Franklin helps cure this problem in her article Fun and Inspiring Poems with Figurative Language for Elementary Students".

Figurative language is a great way to build critical thinking skills and help young learners understand different ways to communicate. Her article provides a great selection of CommonLit texts that come with engaging activities to help elementary students jump into figurative language with a SPLASH.

Maybe you've picked up on which story I checked out from the article? If not, head back over to CommonLit and check out Carolyn Dee Flores's poem, Thank You, Sleep. It's an engaging read and will help your young learners understand figurative language like onomatopoeias and even imagery.
